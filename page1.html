My Rock Climbing Experience

Top Roping

Top roping is where I started climbing and what I thought people meant by 'rock climbing' when I heard the phrase. Top roping is where a rope is attached to the top of a large wall and someone at the bottom belays to make sure you don't fall far. I had top roped a few times before when I was a kid and only one or two times as an adult before earlier this year (2022). I had always liked it, but never found a partner to go consistently with until my friend Tyler said that he started going and I asked to come along one time. We had not top roped before and had to complete a test to prove that we could belay for each other. so we found a video on youtube and tried to learn that way. After a while we attempted the test and the person administering it, whose name was Donna, knew we didn't really know what we were doing. A few attempts later she taught us how and with a little practice we figured it out. After that, Tyler and I spent close to an hour trying to climb walls and figuring out which ones we could actually complete. The hardest one I could do on that day was a 5.10B, which is not a high rating, but it felt difficult at the time. The whole experience was very enjoyable and we made it a weekly thing. We have taken a couple breaks in the several months that we have been climbing for various reasons, but we still go climbing together pretty regularly.

Bouldering

Bouldering is different than top roping in that the walls are much lower and you don't need a partner to belay for you. Tyler and I would occasionally boulder for short times during our sessions, but since top roping is an activity that requires two people, most of the time we find ourselves doing that. Outside of those times I would find myself wanting to climb with not partner, so I would go to the gym and boulder for an hour or so a couple times a week. There was a month or so that I would go three to four times a week. I loved bouldering by myself, as I could just listen to music and try to finish routes that were challenging and required many attempts. I improved a decent amount to the point where I could do some v4s, but then I hit a wall. A month or so later, I was still stuck climbing v4s and I lost quite a bit of motivation to go. I would still go once a week with Tyler, but I didn't feel the same motivation to go by myself. Then I started to get my family to come with me.

Getting Others to Join

A few months ago I started to get my family to come along with me and try rock climbing. I started with my brother because I had been talking about it for a while and he had expressed some interest in going. So we went bouldering a couple times and he liked going with me. After a few times we made it an almost weekly occurance, just like I had with Tyler. This helped me get some of the motivation back to go and improve. I still go with my brother every week and have tried to get other people to come with me. I have succeeded in getting my fiance, and my dad to join me once, but I'm always looking for more people to go with.
